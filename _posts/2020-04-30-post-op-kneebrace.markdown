### Overview

We designed an active post-op knee brace that enables the user to exercise their leg within the Range of Motion (RoM) recommended by their physician, thereby ensuring a proper recovery. The RoM is set via the two buttons on the side of the brace. When the user's leg moves out of the RoM, detected by a potentiometer, a motor gently pushes their leg back to ensure they remain within the RoM.

The problem is that current post-op recovery knee braces do not provide any user data or active feedback. This is important because improper usage of the brace can result in prolonged recovery time and swelling in the lower leg. Currently available designs, though they hold the knee in place and restrict movement efficiently, fail to record range of motion (RoM) as they only restrict RoM. Post ACL-operation patients need a more convenient and accurate way to precisely adjust their knee brace’s range of motion according to their doctor’s specifications. Design a durable, intuitive, accessible, and cost-effective SparkFun knee brace add-on system that will measure RoM, adjust acceptable RoM, and correct RoM with active force with quantifiable constraints such as being no larger than 12”x18”x12”, costing less than $50, and being within 1-2 pounds in weight.
